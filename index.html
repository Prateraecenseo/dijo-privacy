Why DiJo

Most note-taking apps want to be a platform. DiJo wants to be a notebook — bounded, quiet, and entirely yours.

What's inside

Few features, made well.

Everything paper does, plus the handful of things paper can't. Nothing more.

Pressure-sensitive ink

Apple Pencil renders every stroke exactly as hard as you draw it. Four pen sizes, a highlighter, an eraser, and a built-in ruler.

Three classic pages

Dot grid, ruled, or blank — in A5 and A6, at near-actual-size proportions. 251 pages to a notebook.

Tap-to-jump index

A topic index with page references and ranges. Tap an entry and land on the page. No flipping, no search box.

A real page turn

The A6 landscape spread lifts only the inner page over the spine — the way a real notebook turns, not stretched or faked.

Locked to your iPad

Each notebook can sit behind Face ID or your passcode. More private than a paper notebook left on a desk.

A shelf of your own

Build a library of notebooks. Per-page undo and redo that's never destructive. Export any page as PDF or image.

Privacy by architecture

Your notes never leave your iPad.

This isn't a privacy policy. It's how the app is built. There is no server for your words to travel to.

  • No account. Nothing to sign up for, nothing to sign in to.
  • No cloud sync. Your notebooks live on the device, not on ours.
  • No analytics. The app doesn't measure you. Apple confirms: no data collected.
  • No advertising. No trackers, no third parties, no exceptions.
  • No AI assistant. Nothing reads your pages or suggests what to write.
  • Your backup, your choice. Optional iCloud Drive backup that you control.

Questions

Good to know.

Does DiJo sync between my devices?

No — and that's deliberate. DiJo keeps your notebooks on the iPad they were made on. If you want a safety copy, you can turn on optional iCloud Drive backup, which stays inside your own iCloud account. Nothing passes through a DiJo server, because there isn't one.

What happens to my notes if I change iPad?

If you use the optional iCloud Drive backup, restoring your new iPad from an iCloud backup brings your notebooks with it. Your data follows your Apple account — not DiJo.

Do I need an Apple Pencil?

Yes. DiJo's writing experience is built around the Apple Pencil's pressure-sensitive ink — an Apple Pencil is required to use the app.

Is there a free trial?

No trial, and no free tier with features held back. DiJo is a single one-time purchase of $5.99. You buy the whole app once and own it.

Will there be a subscription later?

No. DiJo is sold as a one-time purchase on principle. Updates within DiJo 1.x are included.

Is it on iPhone or Mac?

DiJo is iPad-only. It was designed for the iPad form factor and Apple Pencil from the first day, rather than stretched across devices it wasn't built for.
